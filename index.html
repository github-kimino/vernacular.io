What is Vernacular HTML?

HTML is one of the most widespread and interoperable data formats available. It is extremely versatile and flexible, and everyone has a way of seeing. Combined with JavaScript, CSS, SVG, and the rest of the Web platform, it can be used as a very powerful format for pretty much everything.

These are highly desirable properties for domain-specific data formats, but out of the box they come at a cost. The domain‘s applications will want to consume data that is somewhat more predictable than fully general HTML, and to do so interoperably.

Vernacular HTML is the practice of building domain-specific languages on top of HTML, fully compatible with it, for the purpose of addressing the needs of a specific domain in an interoperable manner. It is not a technology so much as a body of good practices.
